Powerbuilding Split

Essential Information: Dynamic Stretches and Proper Warm Ups

Brief description of what warmups for each day should look like essentially depending on the athlete's goal. ]

Monday - Squats + Benchpress

Essentially a strength day for the Benchpress and heavy volume day for legs. Follow the link for the assigned program for this day.

Tuesday - Deadlifts

Essentially a strength day for deadlifts with a top single followed by 3 sets of lowered weight depending on the week in the program itself. Follow the link for the assigned program for this day.

Wednesday - Benchpress

Essentially the most important day for increasing overall strength in this exercise. Follow the link for the assigned program for this day.

Thursday - Squats

Raw power for squats for this block. Top doubles and top singles depending on week in program. Follow the link for the assigned program for this day.

Friday - Bench and Deadlifts

The bench program for this day is slightly different involving a lot of controlled repetitions instead of pushing as much weight as possible. In terms of deadlifts, it is leaning more towards a volume and form day to minmimize injury. Follow the link for the assigned program for this day. Ultimately the rest of the weekend (Saturday and Sunday) will be rest days in order to allow the Central Nervous System (CNS) to reset. This program is designed for strength and size so your CNS will need proper recovery. One of the main indicators that your CNS isn't receiving enough recovery is a consistent down trend in strength throughout this program. Obviously strength is not going to always be linear and increase in that way and you may run into a specific week where progress is slow or no progress is happening. But that shouldn't be the case for every week. This program is designed to help and not hurt.
